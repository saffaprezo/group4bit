A group of Child Advocacy Organization continue engagement in the fight against COVID 19

(Children Education Forum (CEF-SL), Children Ambassador Kenema, Children for Brighter Future (CBF), Voice of the Children (VOC) As the COVID 19 pandemic disease is on the increase in Sierra Leone and the world at large, a group of Child advocate from three strong child advocacy organization in kenema, continue to do Media Sensitization on the preventive measure and still encourage citizens to observe all the measures giving be the health as this we will reduce the spread of the disease in the country. The national coordinator of the Children Education Forum, Mr Ibrahim Saffa also call on the attention of all citizens, especially school pupils to pay great attention to the radio teaching program as this will help them to involve into research work by themselves and ask educated people in there communities for assistance or directions towards the process, Mr Sorie A Dumbuya President of Children Ambassador Kenema, also said that besides are all of the fact that there are a lot of challenges in the process, but therefore, to promote education and to help prevent drop out rate in the country, he encourage parents to monitor there children to participate in the radio teaching program as it will them a lot, and promote there ademic intelligence. Regiatu the vice president of the voice of the Children Sierra Leone, as commended the effort of the government of Sierra Leone and the Ministry of Education for the implementation of the radio teaching program, but she also reminding the government and the Ministry of education about the negatives impact of the radio teaching program more especially in the area of practical subjects for the Science students, and she even asked if how this subject are going to deliver over the radio so that it will great an impact to the education of these pupils. Mr Ibrahim Saffa who is the national coordinator for the Children Education Forum called upon all Sierra Leoneans to play there various roles in other to combat the fight against COVID 19, as this fight has to done by everyone. Mr Ibrahim Saffa said in the area of the radio teaching program, there are teachers in almost all of the community in Sierra Leone,therefore he is calling and encouraging them to be volunteering at the commutniies to be helping pupils during the radio teaching program by monitoring there works and examine there progress as well, till the pandemic disease comes to an end.

A community youth organization disturbed 300 maske to it community members to help fight Corona Virus

A Community Youth Base Organization Called KAKAJAMA YOUTH DEVELOPMENT ASSOCIATION KAYDA has on Sunday 10th of May embarked on community Sensitization, Public Health Education, and Distribution of 356 face masks to the people of Kakajama to help fight against the Corona virus in our Beloved Country MAMA SALONE. President Bio says improvement in science and technology is fundamental to development

President Bio says improvement in science and technology is fundamental to development* *_Mile 91, Tonkolili District, Friday 29 November 2019_* – His Excellency President Dr Julius Maada Bio has told students at the Canadian College of Modern Technology in Mile 91 that improvement in science and technology is fundamental to the development of the country. In his welcome address, Chief Executive Officer of the college, Muckson Sesay, said that they were pleased to receive the President, noting that that was demonstrative of his commitment to human capacity building not only in words but also in action. He said their programmes were good and the classrooms were well-equipped to ensure students got the required skills as a means of integrating technology into the syllabi. Minister of Information and Communication, Mohamed Rahman Swaray, reiterated that the President was visiting the college because of his commitment to education and inclusive development and commended the administration of the college for providing modern facilities that would help to capacitate young Sierra Leoneans. In his remarks, President Bio said that he was happy to be at the campus, saying that the work of the college was in line with his vision for human capital development. He said he wanted to leave a lasting legacy in the country by building on education that would be a foundation for sustainable development and the improvement of the wellbeing of citizens. He mentioned that the college was offering the best to students that would enable them to be competitive in the challenging world, noting that he wanted to produce quality Sierra Leoneans that would help the growth of the country and the world at large. He therefore urged students to make good use of the facilities. ‘’I am pleased with the type of education offered here in technology and enterprenuership because of its quality and relevance to society. I want to use this opportunity to thank the CEO and sponsors for bringing back to society. We will give him our full support and encourage the community to also do the same,’’ he said.
